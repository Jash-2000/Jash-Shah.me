01 — About

Engineer at the Hardware–Software Boundary

I'm a B.E. (Hons.) Electronics & Instrumentation graduate from BITS Pilani, Pilani Campus. My work lives at the intersection of physical systems and intelligent software — designing hardware that senses, computes, and acts in the real world.

What drives me is the challenge of multi-disciplinary engineering: combining low-level firmware development with high-level system design, sensor physics with machine learning, and control theory with real-time computing. I've built autonomous robots, FPGA-based signal processors, industrial IoT nodes, and intelligent HMI systems — always with a focus on making things that work reliably in practice, not just in simulation.

"Be a game changer — the world is already full of players."

Beyond engineering, I'm a hardcore patriot with a long-horizon goal: to establish a premier R&D centre in India that attracts the best minds and builds technology that competes globally. I hold a place in the Malaysian Book of Records for the Grade 11 International Standard of Abacus Computation (2009).

03 — Research

Research & Ideas

A mix of published work, ongoing investigations, and conceptual research directions I've developed. Good research starts with a well-formed question — these are mine.

Published / Presented
Real-Time Embedded Control for Autonomous Navigation in Unstructured Environments
Explored the co-design of embedded hardware and control algorithms for robots operating in GPS-denied, unstructured spaces. Proposed a hybrid reactive–deliberative architecture enabling faster obstacle response without sacrificing global path optimality.
Jash Shah · BITS Pilani Control Systems · Embedded
Ongoing
Sensor Fusion for Robust State Estimation Under Noisy Conditions
Investigating Kalman Filter and Particle Filter based sensor fusion frameworks for combining IMU, LIDAR, and visual odometry data. Focus on achieving reliable pose estimation at low computational cost on embedded platforms.
Jash Shah Robotics · Signal Processing
Research Concept
Decentralised Swarm Intelligence for Disaster Search-and-Rescue
Proposed a heterogeneous robot swarm architecture for post-disaster environments where communication is unreliable. Agents share discovered state via stigmergy-inspired markers, enabling collective mapping with no single point of failure.
Jash Shah Swarm Robotics · Multi-agent Systems
Research Concept
Cognitive Load Reduction in Industrial HMI via Adaptive UI
Theoretical framework for HMI systems that adapt their information density and layout in real time based on operator cognitive load metrics (eye tracking, response latency, EEG). Aimed at reducing critical errors in high-stakes industrial control environments.
Jash Shah HMI · Cognitive Science
Ongoing
Low-Power Neural Network Inference on Microcontrollers (TinyML)
Investigating model compression techniques — quantisation, pruning, and knowledge distillation — to deploy ML inference on Cortex-M4/M7 class MCUs with <1mW average power. Target applications: wearable biosensing and autonomous edge nodes.
Jash Shah TinyML · Embedded AI · IoT
Research Concept
Haptic Feedback Rendering for Remote Surgical Robotics
Exploring force-feedback algorithms and actuator design for teleoperated surgical robots where latency must remain below the perception threshold (<10ms). Combines impedance control, predictive haptic rendering, and custom piezo actuator arrays.
Jash Shah Haptics · Medical Robotics · Control
Research Concept
EEG-Controlled Prosthetic Limb with Intent Prediction
Concept for a non-invasive BCI-driven prosthetic system using EEG motor imagery classification. Aims to predict intended movement 200–300ms before muscle activation, enabling more natural prosthetic limb behaviour for amputees.
Jash Shah BCI · Medical Instrumentation · ML
Ongoing
Formal Verification of Safety-Critical Embedded Software
Applying model-checking and static analysis tools to verify real-time embedded software for automotive and aerospace applications. Goal: automated proof of freedom from runtime errors on MISRA-C compliant codebases.
Jash Shah Formal Methods · Safety Systems · Embedded
